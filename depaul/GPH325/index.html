Summary

(Formerly GPH 371) A survey of basic 3D techniques, including:

  • Interaction of light and color
  • The relationship between visual effect and geometry
  • Visual effects of rendering, texturing and lighting algorithms
  • Procedurally based modeling and an introduction to procedural animation techniques


What to Expect

This is an upper-level course that assumes you have well-developed skills in program development and debugging. This course is time-intensive and will require significant problem solving effort.

When asking for assistance, you will need to include a description of how the visual aspects of your image deviate from what was expected. The help will come as suggestions on how to debug your code, but the debugging is your responsibility. The cliche 'immature developer' approach of emailing a question to the instructor with the statement "It doesn't work" is not the correct approach to this class, and will not receive a response.


Prerequisites

CSC 212 or CSC 262
It is extremely important that you have either prerequisite before entering this class or have programming experience. This class will not cover basic programming skills.
