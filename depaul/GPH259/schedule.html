Topics

Constructive Geometry (2 weeks)
Students will discuss the evolution of constructive geometry from pre-historic rope geometry to the Greek invention of axiomatic geometry. In this regard students will learn the basic principles of geometry as practiced in the layout and design of such ancient monuments as Stonehenge, Egyptian pyramids, Hindu and Greek temples. Lab exercise will include the compass and straightedge construction of perpendiculars, angle bisection, determining arc centers, triangulating procedures, and the application of right triangles to proto-trigonometric problems.

Vector Geometry (1 week)
Students will learn how constructive principles of geometry are practiced in computer graphics by means of vector representation. Special focus will be given to orthographic coordinate systems. Students will be introduced to the 2D CAD environment by using it to execute advanced constructions.

Polygons and Polyhedrons (1 week)
Students will learn how to generate symmetry transformations in vector environments. Students will generate patterns for creating polyhedrons using the edit commands in CAD.

Trigonometry Basics (1 week)
Students will be introduced to the principles of trigonometry as computational geometry including the derivation of trigonometric functions from the unit circle. Students will learn to solve triangles using these functions.

Projection Geometry (2 weeks)
Students will develop a practical knowledge of projection especially perspective projection and learn to relate perspective to computer rendering. Students will learn the basic principles of perspective projection as developed by DaVinci, Della Francesca and Durer. They will learn to distinguish between conical and cylindrical projection and will see the derivation of conic curves from the projection of the circle.

Curves and Surfaces (2 weeks)
This portion of the class will address the history and development of spline geometry and the application of normals, tangents and different concepts of curvature to analyze these curves. These principles will expand to include NURBS surface geometry, especially as regards the relationships between curves and surfaces. The role of mesh geometry in the rendering of surfaces concludes this section.

Fractals (1 week)
Students will learn the basic principles of fractal geometry and chaos structure as well as their applications in computer graphics.
