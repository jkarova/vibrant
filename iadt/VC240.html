Course Description

Students explore the principles of interactivity and animation on the computer. Upon completion of this course students should be able to understand and apply the principles of creating basic electronic storyboards and interactive presentations incorporating text, sound, images and video on the computer.

Weekly, class will be structured as follows: Concepts will be introduced on the first class of the week during a lecture and demonstration, students will then be assigned a project based on this material. The following class will be a lab where students may work on the assignment, which are due at the beginning of class Monday the week after they are assigned. No late work is accepted.

This page serves as the outline and schedule for the class. Having read this page, you should understand the requirements of the class.

Plagiarism

Plagiarism can be a difficult concept to define; however, simply put, plagiarism is using other people’s ideas and words without clearly acknowledging the source of that information. It is important to note that in college, we are continually exposed to other’s ideas. We read ideas and words in textbooks, hear them in lectures, discuss them in class, and incorporate them into our own writing. One must always keep in mind that you must give credit when credit is due.

In order to avoid plagiarism, you must give credit whenever you use:

  • Another person’s ideas, opinion or theory
  • Any facts, statistics, graphs, visual images (i.e. drawings, videos, etc.) that are not common knowledge
  • Quotations of another person’s spoken or written words; or
  • Paraphrase of another person’s spoken or written words

Students who are in violation of IADT’s plagiarism policy may be subject to the following:

  • Dismissal from the course in which the plagiarism occurred
  • Receive a failing grade for the course
  • Placed on academic probation for a quarter
  • Dismissal from the Academy

Assignments, Midterm & Final Project

Weekly assignments:

  • Assignment 1: Create an animation of a landscape passing by, as though you were in a car.
  • Assignment 2: Create an animation of a clock flying through the sky.
  • Assignment 3: Create an animation of the solar system which uses nested clips to show the orbit of the planets and some of their moons. Bonus points for better detail. Present your swf file embedded in HTML.
  • Assignment 4: Improve the movie submitted for assignment 3 to incorporate bitmaps of the planets (to replace the shapes in your planet_gf movieclips) and event handling to indicate when the mouse has rolled over and out of the sun, either through an indicator movie (like Fred in class), or by changing the sun's color. If you choose to get more complicated, add some event handling for mouse activity over the orbits of the various planets, changing their color when the mouse rolls over their orbit.
  • Assignment 5: Choose a theme and create a sound board for that theme featuring buttons and some animation.
  • Assignment 6: Create a movie that incorporates either a sound or video clip.

Midterm: Create a map of the solar system that identifies each body and it's orbit. When you rollover a planet's orbit, it's orbit should change color. When you click on the planet's orbit, a text field in the movie should give some information on the planet: it's name, it's moons, etc...

Final Project: Create an animation of a character moving through a landscape, similar to what you've seen while playing an old-style video game, like Super Mario Bros. This animation must possess a button that switches the character from a walk to a run, and buttons to allow the character to jump and duck to avoid obstacles in the environment. While running, the landscape behind the character must move twice as fast as when the character is walking. See the character.fla file for an example.
