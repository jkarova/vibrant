An Intimate Dinner with Alice Waters

Enjoy a harvest feast and mingle with Alice Waters. Slow Food Chicago and Kendall College present a dining experience to benefit Ms Waters' foundation, Slow Food Nation, and Slow Food Chicago.

Attendees will have the chance to meet Ms Waters and enjoy a wine reception with passed canapes, followed by a five course dinner featuring Midwest artisanal produce and products. Funds from the event will support the Slow Food Nation Campaign and help Slow Food Chicago send representatives from the Midwest to the event in San Francisco in May of 2008.

Date: Friday, October 5th
Time: 6:00pm Wine, Cocktails & Canapes, 7:30pm Five Course Dinner with Wines
Location: The Dining Room of Kendall College, 900 N. Branch St., just North of Chicago Ave. and N. Halsted St. (Google Map)
Admission cost: $150. Proceeds benefits Slow Food Nation and Slow Food Chicago. Space is limited to 100 attendees. Reserve today!
To Reserve: Click here. Online registration provided by ChicagoCooks.com

Slow Food Nation is a year long campaign whose goal is to raise America’s awareness for how food is produced and distributed in the United States. The campaign will demonstrate how everyday choices affect our well being, our culture, and the health of the planet. Slow Food Nation's goal combines pleasure with responsibility to inspire a new activism with food at its core. The campaign will peak with a four-day exposition in San Francisco in May of 2008 that will bring together farmers, producers, chefs, and foodies to participate in discussions, lectures, and tastings in order to promote and celebrate American Artisanal foods.
